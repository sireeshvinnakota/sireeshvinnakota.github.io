Calculus II — §11.5 Alternating Series Test
← Back to Calc II graph  

Theorem (Alternating Series Test)
If the alternating series: $$ > \sum_{n = 1}^\infty \left ( -1 \right )^{n-1} b_n = b_1 - b_2 + b_3 - \cdots \quad \text{ with } \quad b_n > 0 > $$ satisfies the following conditions:
  • $b_{n+1} \leq b_n$ for all $n$ and
  • $\lim_{n \to \infty} b_n = 0$
Then the series converges.
Theorem (Alternating Series Estimation)
If we have the alternating series $S = \sum (-1)^{n-1} b_n$ where $b_n >0$ satisfying $(i)$ and $(ii)$, then $$ > \left | R_n \right | = \left | s - s_n \right | \leq b_{n+1} > $$

From the picture, we see that the terms in absolute value get close to the limit.

Example
Consider the alternating harmonic series: $$ > \sum_{n = 1}^\infty \frac{(-1)^n}{n} > $$ Determine whether this series diverges, and if so, determine the remainder at the $n = 5$ term.
Solution
We’ll show that this series satisfies the conditions from Theorem:
  • We know that: $$\frac{1}{n+1} < \frac{1}{n} \checkmark$$
  • We also have that: $$\lim_{n \to \infty} \frac1n = 0 \checkmark$$
Thus, by Theorem , we have convergence. To estimate at the $5$th term, we see: $$ \left | s - s_5 \right | \leq \frac{1}{5 + 1} \quad \text{ and thus } \quad -\frac16 \leq s - s_5 \leq \frac16 $$ Substituting for what we know, we have: $$ s_5 = \sum_{n = 1}^5 \frac1n = \frac{-47}{60} $$ This gives: $$ -\frac16 \leq s + \frac{47}{60} \leq \frac16 \quad \text{ so } \quad -\frac16 - \frac{47}{60} \leq s \leq \frac16 - \frac{47}{60} $$
Problem
Determine if the following series converges or diverges: $$ > \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^{n-1}}{7+2 n} > $$
Solution
The sequence $\frac{1}{7 + 2n}$ is decreasing and has limit = 0, so converges by Theorem
